First let's start off by explaining what pyweek is.
Pyweek is a bi-annual (twice a year) event that is held by the current developers/maintainers of python, pyweek allows users to create a game based of the winning catergory that the contenders choose. There are usually 5 catergories that the contenders can pick from. In pyweek35, the pyweek myself and RangerRhino took part in, the choices were as follows:

•In the shadows
•Las Vegas Nights
•Mass-produced
•Left, right, down
•Re-entry

Mine and RangerRhino's entry

and after casting out votes and waiting for the voting to end the victorious catergory was In the shadows which lead to us making Echoes in the Dark which lead to us getting 2.9/4 and scoring quite well. And we will be part-taking in the next pyweek

How to sign up.

To sign up for pyweek you must go to this website and create an account for free. Once you have made your account you can now wait 'till the next pyweek has started and enter your game after the week is up. Make sure to familiarize yourself with all the rules regarding pyweek so you don't get disqualified. You can read about the rules here.

Engines you can use.

There are a lot of engines you can use when developing a game for pyweek, some of the most popular are:

• Pygame
• Ursina
• Panda3D

And loads more Be careful when using different engines as they may not be supported. But other than that you can create anything you want, thats in line with the rules.

Side notes

Some side notes include

- Making sure you plan your game out
- Voting for a catergory
- If you have assets before pyweek has started make sure to link them
- Have fun

In conclusion

Pyweek is one of the biggest python game jams out there hosted by the offical maintainers of python and the offical python discord server. Pyweek has been going on 17 and a half years (Thats 2005!) and continues to be held twice a year to this day. Have fun programming!
